# Research Participation Opportunity — Extra Credit

Dear Students,

You are invited to participate in a research study on how people learn to recognize hidden patterns. This study is being conducted by Dr. Kiran Boggavarapu in the Department of Chemistry and Physics at McNeese State University.

## What is the study about?

We are investigating how people discover rules in sequences of letters. Some people figure out the pattern suddenly, while others learn gradually. We are developing new tools to measure these differences, which may help us understand how learning works across many domains — from classroom problem-solving to scientific discovery.

## What would I do?

You will complete a 25–30 minute online task on your computer or phone:

1. Study a set of letter strings that follow a hidden rule
2. Classify new strings as following or not following the rule
3. Rate your confidence periodically
4. Answer a brief survey at the end

The task is similar to a puzzle game. There are no right or wrong answers expected at the start — learning happens during the task itself.

## How do I participate?

Go to:

Complete the study in one sitting on a desktop or laptop computer (recommended) with a stable internet connection. Please keep the browser tab in the foreground during the study phase.
