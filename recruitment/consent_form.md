# Informed Consent Form

## Study Title
Pattern Learning and Insight in Sequence Classification

## Investigators
[Your name and affiliation]

## Purpose
You are invited to participate in a research study investigating how people learn
to recognize patterns in letter sequences. We are interested in understanding when
and how learners experience moments of sudden understanding ("aha" moments).

## What You Will Do
- View sequences of letters on a computer screen
- Judge whether each sequence follows a hidden pattern (yes/no)
- Receive feedback on some of your judgments
- Rate your confidence in your understanding periodically
- Press a button if/when you feel you understand the pattern
- Answer a brief survey about your experience

**Duration**: Approximately 30-40 minutes in a single session.

## Risks
This study involves minimal risk. You may experience mild frustration if the patterns
are difficult to learn, similar to playing a puzzle game. You may stop at any time.

## Benefits
You will gain experience participating in cognitive science research. If enrolled
through a course, you will receive [extra credit / course credit] as specified by
your instructor. The study contributes to our understanding of human learning and insight.

## Compensation
- Course participants: [Extra credit amount, as specified by instructor]
- University participants: $10 gift card upon completion
- Online participants (Prolific): $8 upon completion (~$12-16/hour rate)

Partial compensation may be provided if you withdraw after completing at least
50% of the study.

## Confidentiality
Your data will be identified by a random code, not your name. De-identified data
may be shared publicly for scientific transparency (e.g., on the Open Science Framework).
No identifying information will be published.

## Voluntary Participation
Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time without
penalty. Withdrawal will not affect your grade, academic standing, or relationship
with the university.

## Data Collection
We collect: your responses (grammatical/ungrammatical judgments), reaction times,
confidence ratings, button presses, and survey responses. We also collect basic
demographics (age, gender, education level). We do NOT collect your name, email,
IP address, or any personally identifying information.
