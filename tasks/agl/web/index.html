Informed Consent to Participate in Research

Title: Learning Transitions in Pattern Recognition

Overview

You are being asked to take part in a research study. The purpose is to investigate how people learn to recognize hidden patterns in letter sequences, and whether moments of sudden understanding have measurable signatures in behavior.

What you will do

You will complete a 25-30 minute online task: viewing letter sequences, deciding whether each follows a hidden rule, rating your confidence every 10 trials, and answering a brief survey at the end. You may also press a button if/when you feel you understand the pattern.

Voluntary participation

Your participation is voluntary. You do not have to participate and may stop at any time. There will be no penalties or loss of benefits if you do not participate or decide to stop. Your decision will not affect your grade, class standing, or relationship with the university. If extra credit is offered, an equivalent alternative assignment is available.

Risks and benefits

This research is considered minimal risk — the same as risks you face in daily life. You may experience mild frustration if the patterns are difficult, similar to a puzzle game. You will gain experience participating in cognitive science research.

Data collection

We collect: your responses (pattern judgments), response times, confidence ratings, self-reported insight moments, and brief survey responses (age, perceived difficulty, strategy description). Your data is identified by a randomly generated code, not your name. We do NOT collect your name, email, IP address, or any personally identifying information.

Data sharing

De-identified data may be shared publicly for scientific transparency (e.g., on the Open Science Framework or in a data repository accompanying a publication). No identifying information will be included.

Privacy

It is possible, although unlikely, that unauthorized individuals could gain access to your responses because you are responding online. Confidentiality will be maintained to the degree permitted by the technology used. No guarantees can be made regarding the interception of data sent via the Internet. Your participation involves risks similar to everyday use of the Internet.

Before we begin

This study is part of an active research project investigating how people discover hidden patterns — the kind of "aha" moment you may have experienced in chemistry or physics.

Your responses are being used in a real scientific analysis. Each answer matters. Random or rushed responses make the data unusable and waste the opportunity.

The task takes about 25 minutes. Please find a quiet spot, stay on this tab, and give each string your honest best judgment.

As a science student, you know that good data is the foundation of good research. Thank you for contributing yours.

How it works

Step 1: Study

First, you'll see a set of letter strings one at a time. These strings all follow a hidden pattern. Try to memorize them — pay attention to which letters appear and in what order.

Step 2: Test

Then, you'll see new strings and decide if each one follows the same pattern as the ones you studied.

  • Press Follows Pattern or Doesn't Follow
  • You'll get feedback during the learning phase
  • Every 10 trials, rate how confident you feel
  • If you think you've figured out the rule, hit the AHA! button

Don't worry if it's hard at first — that's normal.
